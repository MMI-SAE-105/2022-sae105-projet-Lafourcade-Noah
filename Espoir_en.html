A REBUILD FUTURE

Young people, the solution for a better world

Although they consider themselves very informed about environmental issues (self-assessment 7/10), young people still admit that they are unaware of the issue and the impact of human activity on the planet and do not seek to find out more.They are also aware of a low level of daily engagement (4/10 during the self-assessment of their specific actions).

For them, environmental "efforts" are mainly focused on consumption. Thus, they cite purchasing power as the first lever for environmental action. Still dependent on their parents, they don't feel guilty for not taking action because they don't have the financial capacity to do so.

Group of young people on their phones

As said before, even if some do “nothing” to change the current state of the planet, there are still some who do everything they can to keep doing a little thing. Young people are aware that today’s world is in danger and that they have a role to play in transforming the environment. Far from being passive victims, they are more responsible on the subject. The proof is in the action of the Swedish Greta Thunberg. An environmental activist, in 2018 she spearheaded the global youth movement: Friday’s for future.

Therefore, this generation of social and instant networks requires fast and specific responses. She is ready to change their daily lives and adopt eco-friendly actions to save the Earth. His intentions of our generation are therefore encouraging and positive for the future. It remains to be hoped that this will continue!
