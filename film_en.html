WOLF CHILDREN

A film, a mangaka and a work of art…

Mamoru Hosoda, Japanese director and animator has created a multitude animated films like Summer Wars or "the crossing of time" including the best known and most appreciated of all, "the wolf children". Released in 2012, almost 2 hours long, the film quickly received awards for its original story and its animation. The film is genreed as drama and fantasy cinema

Unlike his other films, this one is presented to us in a contemporary Japan with all its landscapes, from the dynamic city to a peaceful countryside with many scenes which reinforces the realistic side of the film, but the characters themselves are straight out of 'a fairy tale. And it is the mix of the two genres, realism and fantasy that characterizes this film.

Image of the Wolf Children poster

A competitor for a masterclass

amoru Hosoda has created many films being compared to Studio Ghibli by the community, it's no wonder when you notice that the director has taken up the same lessons like stories of impossible love or even dramatic friendships. However, Mamoru Hosoda manages to stand out well and to offer an original and gripping story and leaving every minute of his film with extreme attention whether through action, music or even animation. The lines, the colors taken from a fairly simple panel with round eyes and well-marked expressions, plunge us into a magical world that would make anyone fall in love with this atmosphere.
